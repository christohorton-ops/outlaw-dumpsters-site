Our Story — From the Founder

Why Outlaw Exists

I'm Chris — born and raised in Utah, and the guy who answers the phone at Outlaw. Before starting this company I lived through three home remodels of my own. Two of them I tried to haul to the dump myself in a borrowed trailer. Both times I underestimated the load, paid surprise overage fees, and spent a Saturday I'll never get back at the Lindon dump.

The third remodel I rented from a national chain. They were 6 hours late, dropped the bin on my new concrete without boards, and charged me a "fuel surcharge" that wasn't on the quote. That was the day Outlaw Dumpsters started taking shape on a napkin.

We're licensed and insured in Utah, we put specialized dumpster wheels — rollie skates — under every drop to protect your driveway, and we quote the real price, landfill ticket included, before the truck rolls.

Today we run dumpsters across Utah and Salt Lake Counties for homeowners doing weekend cleanouts, contractors framing new builds in Saratoga Springs, and property managers turning units in Sandy. Every drop is handled by someone who lives within 20 minutes of you.

FAQ

Real Questions, Real Answers

Answered by Chris, owner of Outlaw Dumpsters and a Utah homeowner who's hauled (and rented) his fair share.

Ask Chris Directly
How quickly can you deliver a dumpster in Utah or Salt Lake County?
Same-day delivery is available for most of our service area when you call before noon. We're headquartered in Lehi, so Utah County deliveries are typically our fastest. Salt Lake County is usually next-day but we do same-day there too when we can. Text or call (801) 753-8256 and we'll give you a straight answer on timing.
What size dumpster do I need for a home remodel?
For the vast majority of Utah home projects — bathroom gut, kitchen remodel, full room cleanout, roofing job, or landscaping overhaul — our 15-yard dumpster is exactly right. It holds roughly 4.5 pickup truck loads. If you're unsure, call me and describe the project. I'll tell you honestly whether it fits or if you'd need something different.
What can't I put in the dumpster?
Batteries, tires, and electronics need specific recycling and can't go in. Mattresses are allowed at $25 per mattress — call ahead. Pure concrete-only or pure dirt-only loads aren't accepted, but mixed loads with some concrete or soil are fine. When in doubt, shoot me a text and I'll tell you straight.
How is your pricing structured? Are there hidden fees?
No hidden fees. The $250 flat rate covers delivery, pickup, 5 days of rental, and disposal of up to 2,000 lbs. Additional days are $25 each. Weight overages over 2,000 lbs are 5 cents per pound — and I always call before charging any overage. The price I quote before the truck rolls is what shows up on your receipt.
Will the dumpster damage my driveway?
We use specialized dumpster wheels — what we call rollie skates — under every single drop. They distribute the weight and protect your concrete or asphalt. You don't need to put down plywood or boards. This is something we do as standard, not an upgrade you have to ask for.
